History of Palestine

Late 19th Century

Zionist Movement

The Zionist movement, advocating for a Jewish homeland, gains momentum.

1917

Balfour Declaration

British government expresses support for the establishment of a "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine.

1920s - 1930s

Jewish-Arab Tension

Tensions rise between Jewish and Arab communities in Palestine. Arab protests against Jewish immigration and land purchases lead to violent clashes.

1947

UN Partition Plan

United Nations proposes the partition of Palestine into Jewish and Arab states. Jewish leaders accept; Arab leaders reject.

1948

Israel Independence and Nakba

Israel declares independence.

Arab-Israeli War: Neighboring Arab states invade Israel.

Hundreds of thousands of Palestinians flee or are displaced during the conflict.

1950s

Palestinian Refugees

Palestinians in neighboring countries live in refugee camps. Armistice agreements signed between Israel and its neighbors.

1967

Six-Day War

Six-Day War: Israel occupies the West Bank, East Jerusalem, Gaza Strip, Sinai Peninsula, and Golan Heights. Beginnings of Israeli settlements in the occupied territories.

1978

Camp David Accords

Egypt recognizes Israel, leading to the return of Sinai to Egypt.

1987

First Intifada

Palestinian uprising against Israeli rule begins.

1993

Oslo Accords

Israel and PLO (Palestine Liberation Organization) agree on principles for self-governance in parts of the West Bank and Gaza Strip.
